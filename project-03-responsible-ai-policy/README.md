# Project 3: Responsible AI Policy & Operating Model

## Scenario

**Organisation:** NorthStar Financial Services
**Context:** Following the classification and risk assessment work in Projects 1 and 2, NorthStar's Board has approved the establishment of a formal AI governance programme. The AI Governance Programme Office has been asked to develop two foundational documents: a Responsible AI Policy and an AI Governance Operating Model.

The policy defines what NorthStar believes and commits to. The operating model defines how those commitments are made real.

---

## What This Project Demonstrates

- Understanding that responsible AI principles must translate into **process**, not just statements
- Ability to design a **governance operating structure** — committees, roles, accountability
- Knowledge of how the **NIST AI RMF Govern function** works in practice
- Understanding of the **AI lifecycle** — from use case submission through monitoring
- Ability to connect legal, risk, security, and engineering teams in a coherent governance structure

## Design Principles for This Project

**Principles without process are decoration.** Many organisations publish AI principles that look good on a website but do not change any decision or process. This policy is designed to be operational: every principle has a corresponding process that makes it real.

**Governance is not a bottleneck.** The review process is designed to be proportionate and efficient. Minimal-risk systems get lightweight review. High-risk systems get rigorous review. The goal is appropriate oversight, not maximum friction.

**Structure reflects real organisations.** The operating model is designed for a mid-sized regulated financial services firm — not a tech giant with a dedicated AI ethics team of 50, and not a small startup with no governance. It uses committees and roles that already exist in most organisations, re-purposed for AI oversight.

---

## Frameworks Applied

- **NIST AI RMF** — Govern function (accountability, culture, policies)
- **EU AI Act** — Article 9 (risk management system), Article 14 (human oversight), Article 28–29 (deployer obligations)
- **OECD AI Principles** — Human-centred values, transparency, accountability
- **ISO/IEC 42001** — AI management system requirements (structure and policy alignment)
